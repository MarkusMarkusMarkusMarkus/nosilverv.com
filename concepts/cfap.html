Mission

The Center for Applied Rationality grew from a body of doctrine: the LessWrong Sequences, and the project of taking those lessons and applying them to a life. The premise was that if you reasoned correctly, the rest would follow.

We took those lessons seriously. We also took them out into the wilderness, where many of them failed us. The years since were spent elsewhere — in meditation halls, in bodywork, in practices no respectable rationalist would have endorsed. This center is what we carried back.

The post- is not a rejection. It is what comes after you have internalized the thing and stopped needing to hold it up like a banner. We make no claim to a system. The curriculum is a cabinet of curiosities, assembled by survivorship: each practice earned its place by moving something real in someone. We teach those, and we teach you to notice for yourself when they work.

Selected coursework
Course Catalog
A representative selection. Full prospectus on request.
FAP 101
Foundations of Contraction & Release
Cold showers as a daily diagnostic. Students learn to notice the moment they contract against something — water, information, a person — and to release it.
3 credits · prereq: none
FAP 210
Equanimity Under Provocation
The slapping game as practiced in the equanimity module. Partners work at increasing intensity to study the threshold at which composure gives way, and the interval immediately preceding it.
4 credits · prereq: FAP 101
FAP 240
Affect & the Body (Sarno Method)
A seminar examining the relationship between chronic physical pain and repressed affect. Lecture, followed by structured somatic and verbal release work.
3 credits · corequisite: Group Wailing
FAP 310
Adversarial Epistemology
Seating is assigned to maximize disagreement. The objective is not resolution but the capacity to hold a contested position without contracting against the opposing one.
3 credits · prereq: none
FAP 401
Concentration: Access through Jhana
Sustained absorption practice for the advanced cohort. Fetters work is available to students who have established a stable concentration base.
5 credits · prereq: instructor approval
FAP 420
Voice & Spontaneous Expression
Ultraspeaking, emotional mimicry, and ensemble exercises develop the capacity to speak and respond without rehearsal under observation.
3 credits · prereq: none
