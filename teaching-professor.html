During the PhD in Vienna. Teaching a seminar — something adjacent to the research, something I actually wanted to talk about in a room with people who hadn't made up their minds yet.

Students are different from everyone else I've taught. They push back differently. They're more likely to have read the thing and disagree with it, which is what you want. The seminar format suited me — less lecture, more back and forth, everyone building something together by the end.

It turned out I liked the university more than I expected. The rhythm of it. The excuse to think slowly.
