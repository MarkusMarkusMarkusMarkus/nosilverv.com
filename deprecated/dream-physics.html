The Dream Physics Project · Results

What holds, and what breaks,
in the night.

11 questions · ~65–78 respondents each · collected Aug 2023

In August 2023 I ran a survey on the physics of dreams. It had 11 questions and between 65–78 respondents each. To my knowledge it's cutting edge research on the physics of dreams. I present the results in this page.

Patterns in the data

The dream body is liberated upward. Flying (85%) and jumping high (88%) are nearly universal — but the arm that punches is almost always weak (only 32% can punch hard).

Language is surprisingly intact. More than half can read, and nearly three-quarters can perceive numbers. The symbolic layer of waking life doesn't fully dissolve.

The mundane fails quietly. Light switches, faces in mirrors, stable objects — the ordinary infrastructure of consensus reality is where the seams show.
