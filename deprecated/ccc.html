How it works
01
Stake your presence

Commit €X to be at [PLACE] at [TIME]. The amount is yours to choose — even €5 is enough skin in the game. Your commitment and everyone else's is fully public.

02
Watch the pool grow

As others commit, the total pool grows. You can see exactly how many people are in and how much is at stake. When the threshold is hit, everyone knows the event is happening.

03
Show up, or forfeit

At the time of the event, enable geolocation to confirm you're there. If you show up and others don't, you receive a pro-rata share of their forfeited stakes. This makes showing up the dominant strategy.

Why it actually works

The classic collective action problem: everyone wants the outcome but nobody wants to bear the cost alone. The result is paralysis — even when a clear majority wants to act.

COMMIT resolves this with a mechanism borrowed from venture capital's soft-circling practice: conditional commitments that activate simultaneously. Your commitment only binds if enough others commit too.

Once staked, the dominant strategy shifts. You've already paid the entry cost. Now showing up is free, and no-shows are penalised. The equilibrium is everyone showing up.
